Child Safety Standards

Selecto is a voice calling application built for safe, respectful connections. We maintain zero tolerance for child sexual abuse material and exploitation on our platform.

Effective: 28 June 2026 Last updated: 28 June 2026
Our commitment

Selecto has zero tolerance for child sexual abuse material (CSAM) and any content that sexually exploits, abuses, or endangers children. Protecting children is a core responsibility — not an optional policy.

1. Age requirement

Selecto is intended for users aged 13 years and above. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

If we learn that a user is under 13, we will take steps to delete their account and associated data promptly.

2. Prohibited content & conduct

The following is strictly prohibited on Selecto and will result in immediate action:

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
  • Content that sexually exploits, abuses, or endangers children
  • Grooming or soliciting children for sexual purposes
  • Sextortion or coercion of children
  • Trafficking of children for sex

Users who violate these standards will be permanently removed from the platform and, where applicable, reported to authorities.

3. Reporting mechanism

If you encounter content or behaviour that may put a child at risk, please report it immediately using any of the options below:

📱
In-app report

Use the Report section inside the Selecto app to flag a user or concern.

✉️
Email

nesthamaservices@gmail.com

Include as much detail as possible (username, date, description). All reports are treated confidentially.

4. Our response

When we receive a child safety report, we act quickly and decisively:

  • We investigate reported concerns within 24 hours.
  • Violating content is removed immediately when confirmed.
  • The responsible account is permanently banned.
  • Confirmed CSAM is reported to NCMEC and relevant local law enforcement.

5. Compliance with laws

We comply with applicable child safety laws and work in alignment with recognised international frameworks, including:

NCMEC (US) INHOPE (EU) IWF (UK) NCPCR (India)

We cooperate with law enforcement and trusted safety organisations when required to protect children and prevent further harm.
